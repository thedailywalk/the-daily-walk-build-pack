# The Daily Walk Newsletter — Voiceover Handoff Brief

**Goal:** Add a spoken voiceover to 10 short Scripture reels using **CapCut** (free), then send the finished videos back.

**Time:** ~2–3 minutes per video once you've done the first one. 10 videos total.

**What you're given:** 10 MP4 files (music already included, no voice yet). Each is 1080×1350, ~16 seconds. The graphic finishes drawing at about **9–10 seconds**, then holds still until the end — **that held part is where the voice goes.**

---

## The voice we want

- A **calm, warm, powerful male voice** — think gentle documentary narrator. Unhurried and reassuring, not hyped or salesy.
- In CapCut's Text‑to‑Speech list, good picks are voices named things like **"Deep,"** **"Narration,"** **"Explainer,"** **"Warm,"** or **"Storyteller."** Try 2–3 and pick the warmest, most natural male one. Use the **same voice for all 10** so they feel like a set.
- If CapCut asks about speed, set it **slightly slower** (about ‑10%) for a calm feel.

---

## Step by step (do this for each video)

1. Open **CapCut** → on the left under **"Video editing"** click **Home** → click **New project**. (Do **not** use "Design Studio" / the "Imagine it. Design it." box — that's the AI image tool and it will say "not supported.")
2. Click **Import** (top-left) and choose the video file — or just drag the file into the media panel.
3. **Drag the clip down onto the timeline** (the strip across the bottom).
4. Click on the timeline ruler at about **`00:10`** (10 seconds) so the white playhead line sits there. This is where the voice should start.
5. In the top toolbar click **Text → Add text**. A text box appears on the timeline at the playhead.
6. In the text box, **type the exact line for that video** (see the list below — match it to the file name).
7. With the text selected, open the **Text to speech** section in the right‑hand panel → pick the chosen male voice → click **Save / Generate**. CapCut creates a voice audio clip.
8. **Hide the on‑screen words** (we only want to *hear* it, not see it): with the text selected, in the right panel set **Opacity to 0**. (The voice audio still plays.)
9. Make sure the **voice audio starts around `00:10`** — drag it on the timeline if needed so it begins right as the graphic settles and finishes before the video ends.
10. *(If the music feels too loud under the voice)* click the video/music track → lower **Volume** to about **40–50%**.
11. **Export** (top‑right): **1080p**, **30 fps**, MP4. Save it with the **same file name but starting with `voiced-`** (e.g. `voiced-free-jul09-mark-11-24.mp4`).

Repeat for all 10. After the first one, it's just: new project → import → drop on timeline → change the text line → same voice → export.

## Quick quality check before exporting each one

- ✅ You can **hear** the voice, but the words are **not visible** on screen (opacity 0).
- ✅ The voice **starts around 9–10 seconds** and finishes before the video ends (no cut‑off mid‑sentence).
- ✅ The music is still there, just softer under the voice.
- ✅ Same voice used on every video.
- ✅ Exported at **1080p**, named `voiced-…`.

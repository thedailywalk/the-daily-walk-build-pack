What's Actually Going On

The plain-English version

Most stories introduce their hero by their birth. John doesn't. He reaches all the way back before time and says Jesus — "the Word" — was already there, and was God (verse 1). "The Word" was how people back then described God expressing Himself. John's point: Jesus isn't a character who shows up partway through the story. He's the author who stepped into it.

"The Word became flesh and made his dwelling among us." — John 1:14

That single line is the whole Bible in miniature: God didn't stay distant. He moved into the neighborhood. Then watch what happens next — John the Baptist points to Jesus and calls Him "the Lamb of God" (v29), and the first followers don't get a lecture or a list of rules. Jesus simply says, "Come and see" (v39). They came. They saw. That was the beginning.

What This Shows Us About God

God is not distant, vague, or hard to reach. He came near — on purpose, in person, with a name and a face. He took the first step toward us before we ever took one toward Him. And John says this light "shines in the darkness, and the darkness has not overcome it" (v5). Whatever darkness you're carrying into today, it hasn't won.

Make It Real

For your actual today

Notice how the first disciples started: not by having it all figured out, but by accepting an invitation to "come and see." You don't have to clean yourself up, get your doubts sorted, or feel ready. You just have to show up and look. You did that today by reading Day 1. That counts.

Reflection: Where in your life do you most need to know that God came near — not as an idea, but to you? Tell Him about that one thing.
A Prayer to Begin

Jesus, I'm starting. I don't know everything, and I'm learning I don't have to. Thank You that You came near instead of staying far. Meet me here on Day 1 — and walk with me through this whole year. I'm saying yes to "come and see." Amen.

This Week's Focus: Meet Jesus — who He really is
Today's Reading

The whole reason it was written down

📖 Main: John 19–20  ·  Be real with God: sit honestly with Psalm 27

We started this journey in John for a reason. Before you try to do anything with faith, you need to actually meet Jesus — watch how He talks, how He moves, how He treats people the world had written off. Today we reach the end of John's account, and he stops to tell us exactly why he wrote all of it down:

"But these are written so that you may believe that Jesus is the Messiah, the Son of God, and that by believing you may have life in his name." — John 20:31

That's the whole point. Not information — life. Notice too that the risen Jesus doesn't shame Thomas for his doubt; He steps toward it and says, "Put your finger here." Wherever you are today — all in, unsure, or just curious — that's the Jesus you're being introduced to. One who moves toward honest questions, not away from them.

Make It Real

So what, for today?

Belief in the Bible isn't pretending to be certain. Thomas believed because he was honest about his doubt, not in spite of it. You don't have to have it all figured out to take one real step toward God today.

👉 Where are you waiting to feel certain before you'll take a step? What's one honest sentence you could say to God about that right now?
A Prayer for Today

Jesus, I don't have everything figured out, and I'm learning I don't have to. Thank You for stepping toward my questions instead of away from them. Help me believe — not perfectly, just honestly — and to find real life in You today. Amen.

Pastor's Take · Wednesdays

You can carry a hundred questions to Jesus and still take the next step — that's not weak faith, that's exactly the kind He keeps showing up for. Don't wait until you're sure. Walk, and let the seeing come on the way."
